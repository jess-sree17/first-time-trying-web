KOENIGSEGG

Koenigsegg is a Swedish company that manufactures high-performance sports cars, often referred to as megacars. Founded in 1994 by Christian von Koenigsegg, the company is known for its innovative technology, extreme performance, and luxurious features.


HISTORY: Koenigsegg's history includes milestones like the development of the CC8S, the first car to hold a Guinness World Record for the world's most powerful production engine, and the Agera RS, which set a top speed record in 2017.

KOENIGSEGG JESKO

The Koenigsegg Jesko is a high-performance hypercar from the Swedish automaker, known for its powerful engine, unique transmission, and two distinct variants: the track-focused Jesko Attack and the high-speed Jesko Absolut.

Key Features of the Jesko:

Engine:

A 5.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 1280 horsepower on regular fuel and 1600 horsepower on E85 biofuel.

V8 ENGINE OF JESKO

Transmission:

A 9-speed Koenigsegg Light Speed Transmission (LST) featuring a multi-clutch system for lightning-fast gear changes.


JESKO ENGINE BAY

Variants:

Jesko Attack: Designed for track use, featuring a high-downforce bodywork and active aerodynamics.

Jesko Absolut: Optimized for high speed, with a streamlined design and reduced aerodynamic elements for minimal drag.

Performance:

Jesko Absolut: Capable of reaching a top speed of over 330 mph (530 km/h).

Jesko Attack: Designed for optimal performance on race tracks, with a focus on grip and handling.

Naming:

The Jesko is named after Jesko von Koenigsegg, the father of the company's founder, Christian von Koenigsegg.

Technology:

Features Koenigsegg's UPOD (Ultimate Power On Demand) system, which intelligently selects the best gear for maximum acceleration.

Design:

The Jesko features a redesigned carbon fiber chassis with improved visibility and ergonomics.
